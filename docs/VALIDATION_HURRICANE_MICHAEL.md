## The Anomaly

### Why This Shouldn't Work

According to conventional physics, there is **no causal mechanism** connecting:

1. **Birth chart geometry** (planetary positions at moment of birth)
2. **Future geographic coordinates** (where the subject happens to be decades later)
3. **External weather events** (hurricane formation, trajectory, landfall timing)

Gravitational and electromagnetic effects from planets at birth are orders of magnitude weaker than:
- A nurse walking past the delivery room
- A passing truck outside the hospital
- Local geological variations

### What Makes This Strange

The **geographic specificity** makes the anomaly harder to dismiss:

- If the signal were global (same reading everywhere), it could be attributed to confirmation bias or vague symbolism
- But the signal is **geodetic** — it only appears at coordinates where the event occurred
- The Maryland control (same date, same planetary aspects) showed **zero signal**

This eliminates the "Barnum effect" explanation entirely.

### Possible Explanations

| Hypothesis | Description | Status |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| **Instrumentation Error** | Crisis dates encoded into calculation logic | Ruled out (Maryland control shows null on same date) |
| **Selection Bias** | Only favorable results reported | Ruled out (7 placebo dates return baseline) |
| **Information Anomaly** | Unexplained correlation requiring investigation | **Not ruled out** |

## Limitations & Scope

### What This Study Does Not Claim

1. **No causal mechanism is proposed.** We document correlation, not causation. There is no assertion of planetary influence or metaphysical connection.
2. **No predictive authority is asserted.** Retrodiction ≠ prediction. These findings describe past events, not future ones.
3. **Small-N constraints apply.** Four events + seven placebos justify curiosity and replication, not certainty or decision-making.
4. **This is an anomaly report.** The correct classification is: *"Statistically suggestive, geodetically constrained correlation without known mechanism."*

### What Would Strengthen or Invalidate These Findings

| Outcome | Implication |
|---------|-------------|
| Pre-registered forward test succeeds | Strengthens anomaly hypothesis |
| Pre-registered forward test fails | Weakens anomaly hypothesis |
| Independent replication with different subjects | Strengthens generalizability |
| Blinded analyst protocol shows no effect | Suggests instrumentation error or selection bias |

**Failure is informative.** A negative result in pre-registered replication would be a meaningful scientific outcome, not a disappointment. The goal is to determine whether the correlation is real, not to confirm a belief.
